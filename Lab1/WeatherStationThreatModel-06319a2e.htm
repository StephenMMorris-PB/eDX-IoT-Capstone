Category:Elevation of Privileges

Justification:<no mitigation provided>
Possible Mitigation(s):Enable fine-grained access management to Azure Subscription using RBAC. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtauthz#grained-rbac

3. An adversary may trigger unauthorized commands on the device  [State: Not Started]  [Priority: High] 

Category:Elevation of Privileges
Description:An adversary may leverage insufficient authorization checks on the device and execute unauthorized and sensitive commands remotely.
Justification:<no mitigation provided>
Possible Mitigation(s):Perform authorization checks in the device if it supports various actions that require different permission levels. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtauthz#device-permission

4. An adversary may attempt to intercept encrypted traffic sent to IoT Device  [State: Mitigation Implemented]  [Priority: High] 

Category:Tampering
Description:An adversary may perform a Man-In-The-Middle attack on the encrypted traffic sent to IoT Device
Justification:Customer requires secure sensor telemetry transferred to the cloud. X.509 enrollment to DPS secures device software and optimizes security communications.
Possible Mitigation(s):Verify X.509 certificates used to authenticate SSL, TLS, and DTLS connections. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtcommsec#x509-ssltls

23. An adversary may spoof IoT Device with a fake one  [State: Not Started]  [Priority: High] 

Category:Spoofing
Description:An adversary may replacing the IoT Device or part of the IoT Device with some other IoT Device.
Justification:<no mitigation provided>
Possible Mitigation(s):Ensure that devices connecting to Field or Cloud gateway are authenticated. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtauthn#authn-devices-cloud

24. An adversary may reuse the authentication tokens of IoT Device in another  [State: Not Started]  [Priority: High] 

Category:Spoofing
Description:An attacker may extract cryptographic key material from IoT Device, either at the software or hardware level, and subsequently access the system with a different physical or virtual IoT Device under the identity of the IoT Device the key material has been taken from. A good illustration is remote controls that can turn any TV and that are popular prankster tools.
Justification:<no mitigation provided>
Possible Mitigation(s):Use per-device authentication credentials. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtauthn#authn-cred

25. An adversary may auto-generate valid authentication tokens for IoT Hub  [State: Not Started]  [Priority: High] 

Category:Spoofing
Description:An adversary may predict and generate valid security tokens to authenticate to IoT Hub, by leveraging weak encryption keys
Justification:<no mitigation provided>
Possible Mitigation(s):Generate a random symmetric key of sufficient length for authentication to IoT Hub. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtcrypto#random-hub

26. An adversary may replay stolen long-lived SaS tokens of IoT Hub  [State: Not Started]  [Priority: High] 

Category:Spoofing
Description:An adversary may get access to SaS tokens used to authenticate to IoT Hub. If the lifetime of these tokens is not finite, the adversary may replay the stolen tokens indefinitely
Justification:<no mitigation provided>
Possible Mitigation(s):Use finite lifetimes for generated SaS tokens. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtsmgmt#finite-tokens

27. An adversary may exploit known vulnerabilities in unpatched devices  [State: Not Started]  [Priority: High] 

Category:Tampering
Description:An adversary may leverage known vulnerabilities and exploit a device if the firmware of the device is not updated
Justification:<no mitigation provided>
Possible Mitigation(s):Ensure that the Cloud Gateway implements a process to keep the connected devices firmware up to date. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtconfigmgmt#cloud-firmware

28. An adversary may tamper IoT Device and extract cryptographic key material from it  [State: Not Started]  [Priority: High] 

Category:Tampering
Description:An adversary may partially or wholly replace the software running on IoT Cloud GW (IoT Hub), potentially allowing the replaced software to leverage the genuine identity of the device if the key material or the cryptographic facilities holding key materials were available to the illicit program. For example an attacker may leverage extracted key material to intercept and suppress data from the device on the communication path and replace it with false data that is authenticated with the stolen key material.
Justification:<no mitigation provided>
Possible Mitigation(s):Store Cryptographic Keys securely on IoT Device. Refer: https://aka.ms/tmtcrypto#keys-iot
